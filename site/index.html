About this ledger

Twice a month, the ICCSD school board approves a consent-agenda packet listing every accounts-payable check the district has cut. This site rebuilds two years of those packets — 280 PDF documents from 41 board meetings (June 2024 – June 2026) — into a single searchable dataset.

The source documents

Board Report 10003The master invoice-level report behind each check run: vendor, invoice, PO number, line-item description, and full account code. Present at all 41 meetings; this is the backbone of the ledger (44,280 line items across 102 check runs).
Check registersOne row per physical check, with check numbers and void flags. Published only for the first year; redundant with the board reports, so not loaded.
BMO Mastercard statementsMonthly purchasing-card transaction lists (July 2023 – June 2026). 41,521 card swipes by district staff.
Coded P-Card reportsFor spring 2026, the district began publishing card transactions with account codes and purchase descriptions. Where these match a raw card row, the ledger shows the richer version; April 2026 exists only in this source.
AP summary sheetsOne-page fund totals presented to the board. Used here only to cross-check the parse (see below).

How trustworthy is this?

Every one of the 102 check batches parsed from the board reports sums exactly — to the penny — to the total printed on the district's own report. The table below also compares each batch against the one-page summary sheets the board sees. Where they disagree, the detailed reports are usually right: the summary sheets are assembled by hand and contain occasional copy-paste slips (the Jan 28, 2025 sheet, for instance, omits a $1.45M General-Fund line for the Jan 21 run and lists a stray October date instead).

What's not in here

  • Payroll. Salaries and benefits (the majority of any district's budget) are paid through a separate payroll system and never appear in AP packets.
  • Four small check runs listed on summary sheets whose detailed reports were never uploaded (Oct 22 & 24, 2024; Feb 16 & Mar 24, 2026 — about $6.8M, mostly the missing Oct 22 run).
  • The January 2026 card statement (postings Jan 6–Feb 5) was never published with an agenda, so card spending that month shows artificially low. April 2026 card data comes from the coded P-Card report instead of a raw statement.
  • Card-bill payments (the district paying its own Mastercard balance, ~$183K/month) are excluded so card purchases aren't double-counted or canceled out; 65 unmatched coded rows were dropped as reconciliation noise.

Methodology & caveats

  • Built by parsing the text layer of the district's public PDFs. Dates shown for check runs are the batch dates; card rows use transaction dates.
  • Negative amounts in (parentheses) are credits and refunds.
  • School names for location codes were mined from utility line items (“ELEC – SE” on a bill coded to 0218 names the building). Codes that never appear on a utility bill stay unnamed.
  • Function/object groupings follow Iowa's Uniform Financial Accounting standard at the family level.
  • This is an unofficial, independent rebuild for public understanding — not an audited financial statement.
